Orangutan

Status: critically endangered

Species Facts:

  • There are only 3 species of orangutans, only found on the islands of Borneo and Sumatra.
  • Orangutans make a new nest from tree branches to sleep in each night.
  • The arm spam of an orangutan is nearly one and a half times their height.
  • Orangutans' feet are nearly identical to their hands.
  • The average orangutan lives 30-50 years, spending 7 of those with their mother.
  • Orangutans have been observed using sticks as tools for extracting insects from their mounds.

Endangerment:

  • There is only around 115,000 orangutans left in the wild, 100,000 beloning to only one of the three species.
  • The orangutan population is nearly halved since the year 2000.
  • Deforestation is the greatest contributor to orangutan endangerment.
  • Orangutans are commonly targeted by hunters because their large size and slow speed.
